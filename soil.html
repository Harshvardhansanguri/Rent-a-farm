Alluvial Soil

Found in: Indo-Gangetic plains (Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, Assam)

Regions: Northern plains, River basins

Suitable Crops: Rice, wheat, sugarcane, pulses

Highest MSP Crop: Paddy (MSP ₹2,300/qtl approx.)

Properties: Fertile, rich in potash, needs proper nitrogen supplementation.

Black Soil

Found in: Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh

Regions: Deccan plateau

Suitable Crops: Cotton, jowar, sunflower

Highest MSP Crop: Cotton (MSP ₹6,620/qtl approx.)

Properties: Moisture-retentive, rich in iron, magnesium, calcium.

Red Soil

Found in: Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Odisha, Jharkhand

Regions: Eastern and southern peninsular regions

Suitable Crops: Millets, groundnut, pulses

Highest MSP Crop: Groundnut (MSP ₹6,377/qtl approx.)

Properties: Poor in nitrogen, needs fertilization, good drainage.

Laterite Soil

Found in: Kerala, Karnataka, Western Ghats, Odisha

Regions: Hilly and high rainfall areas

Suitable Crops: Tea, coffee, cashew

Highest MSP Crop: Cashew (Market dependent)

Properties: Acidic, low fertility, porous and loamy.

Mountain Soil

Found in: Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Sikkim

Regions: Himalayan foothills

Suitable Crops: Tea, fruits, spices

Highest MSP Crop: Apple (varies by market)

Properties: Fertile in valleys, rich in organic matter.
